About

Nelson Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully democratic election.

Inspiration on Learning

Mandela believed that education is the foundation of freedom. Listen to one of his speeches about learning:

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.”

Work & Achievements

  • Leader of the African National Congress (ANC)
  • Imprisoned for 27 years for fighting against apartheid
  • First Black President of South Africa (1994–1999)
  • Recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize (1993)
